A number of important things that the banks are checking

There is regulation on the banks, subjected to Bank of Israel guidelines. The Bank of Israel is the one that updates, inter alia, directives regarding mortgages. Such as: shareholders' equity, repayment ratio, entitlements, etc. For this reason, it is important to understand that when we are interested in taking out a mortgage, we must prepare for several main parameters that the Bank examines:


We require at least 25% equity. In other words, if the value of the property is 1 million NIS, we should have at least 250,000 NIS. It is important to know that the higher the percentage of bank financing, the higher the interest rates are, but if the percentage of financing requested by the bank is lower, the interest rates will be lower. It should be noted that if the percentage of financing that the borrowers have is low, it is worthwhile, before signing the purchase contract, to request approval in principle from the bank.


When purchasing a new apartment from the contractor, check whether there is a lending bank for the project. The bank will transfer money to the contractor only after paying the shareholders' equity. Only after payment of shareholders' equity will the bank pay the contractor payments in accordance with the purchase contract. It is important to know - the amounts for financing will be determined according to the assessment of the Bank only. In other words, the percentage of financing will be according to the value determined by the appraiser and not to the amount appearing in the purchase contract.


The monthly payment of the mortgage should not exceed 35% of the net disposable income. (Net income since the banks deduct from the balance of income expenses such as: loans / alimony, etc.). The repayment ratio is 30% to 40% at most (this is a variable that may change in different banks).


It is important to know - a low monthly repayment does not necessarily indicate a mortgage built professionally and properly for the borrower. In most cases, a very low return indicates that the repayment is expected to rise significantly in the future and will also affect the customer's repayment ability in the future, and sometimes even lead to a substantial risk of non-payment. It is also important to understand that the shorter the period, the lower interest rates will be. What is most important is that the mortgage mix will be professionally assembled and customized according to the client's needs.


It is important to ensure that the financial conduct is correct and the credit history of the borrowers is not marked or has alerts.


The Bank is examining the employment continuity of the borrowers. Examines the repayment ability of the borrower to ensure that they can meet payments over time, since the mortgage is a long-term loan.


Therefore, the Bank requests all the relevant documents before submitting the papers and obtaining the approval in principle. There is a difference in submitting a salaried worker’s portfolio to a self-employee’s portfolio. Early Repayment Fee - When do we pay?

You want to repay the mortgage but do not know what commission you will have to pay on early repayment? Let me put it in order and help you understand how you can try to avoid unnecessary costs.



1. Operating commission: a fee that the Bank charges for operating a refund, and amounts to tens of shekels (about 60 NIS).


2. Non-notification fee: The bank asks the borrower for a notice of at least 10 days prior to repayment of the mortgage. If the borrower will update 10 days in advance, he will not pay a commission, but if he does not notify, he will be charged 0.1% of the amount of the mortgage paid.


3. Capitalization commission: (the most significant commission) When we took the loan from the bank, the bank undertook a certain interest at fixed interest (no matter whether it is a fixed interest rates / non-fixed interest / linked or unlinked interest every 5 years or more) Now to repay the loan, the bank will have to find a new borrower for the money, but if the interest rate at early redemption is lower, it means that the bank will benefit less and therefore demands to compensate itself for the difference. Because of these, we have committed ourselves to the bank, to pay a certain interest rate throughout the entire mortgage period, and at the time of early repayment we terminate the liability in the middle of the year.


If so, how do you calculate the commission? When a customer wishes to make early repayment, the bank examines the average interest prevailing at that time in the market on the same loan track (for the remaining period). At the same time, it examines the average interest rate on the same track at the time of taking the loan, or the interest set for the customer - the lower of the two. After the examination, the Bank examines the difference between them:


If the current interest rate on the loan is low or equal to the interest at the time of taking the loan then the client will be charged a capitalization fee. If the interest rate is higher the client will not be charged for the difference.


For example , if you took a 20-year mortgage at a 6% interest rate, which was the average interest rate in the market at the time. After 5 years you asked to refinance the mortgage and make an early repayment. The Bank will examine the average interest on a similar loan for 15 years, to the amount remaining. If the average interest rate stands at 7% for example, the bank will not charge you commission, since the interest rate is higher and the bank can lend your money to someone else without losing. On the other hand, if the interest rate is 4%, the bank will charge you a capitalization commission of 2%.


Another example is that at the exit points defined in the tracks already in advance (for example, every 5 years), we will not pay a capitalization fee at the point of departure (after 5 years), but if we decide to repay the mortgage before the exit point, (For example, if we decide after 3 years to it pay off, we will pay only for the remaining two years to the point of departure). Of course, all this assuming that the interest rate is lower than what we took the mortgage - as I explained in the calculation of the capitalization fee shown above.


Important highlights:


In the tracks on a variable basis for a short period (such as prime, variable each year, foreign currency) there is no capitalization fee because we have pledged that the interest paid will change according to the interest rate changes in the economy.


Eligibility loans do not have capitalization fees because this is a benefit from the state.


4. Average index fee (index-linked loans only):


In the average index fee, in the case in which an index-linked loan is repaid between the 1st of the month and the 15th of the month. Since the index is published on the 15th of each month, in a loan that was repaid in the previous days, the interest rate is actually unknown. Accordingly, the Bank charges a commission at the rate of 50% of the average increase of the previous 12 months (i.e., the borrower will pay half of the average index of the last 12 months). If it turns out that the index for that month was negative, stood at zero, or even positive, but less than the average calculated as the commission, then the customer actually paid a kind of fine.


On the other hand, if you repay the mortgage between the 16th and the end of the month you will not pay a commission, but you have to take into account that on the 15th of that month the published index will be calculated on the mortgage.


It's important to know - if you expect the index to be high in the month you return the mortgage then return it before the 15th, because you will pay only the average index of last year. But if you think the index will be negative or low wait after the 16th of the month.


5. Exchange rate differentials on foreign currency linked loans: (common loans)


An additional commission is collected in the event that a foreign currency-linked loan is taken. The commission is the amount of exchange rate differentials between the foreign exchange rate on the day on which the early repayment was made and the rate that will occur two business days later If the borrower gives written notice of at least two business days Early, he is exempt from this commission.

Dragging a Mortgage - what is the process? And is it profitable?

Mortgage transfer is done when we buy a new property and sell an old one, and the terms in the current mortgage, of the old property, are with excellent conditions. In the act of transferring, we want the mortgage on the old house to be transferred to the new property and the terms of the mortgage to remain the same. Most of us will need to add money to buy the new property. (If the value of the new apartment is lower than the old property, we will need to get permission from the bank to do a transfer, because the value for collateral isn’t as good for the bank).


It's important to know – mortgage transfer can increase your monthly payments, despite the conditions listed. That is not a problem, if your payment ability allows this, but if that is a problem, then the bank might not approve the transfer process and you will have to take a new mortgage for the full amount.


Another important thing to consider is that the bank, in which you are doing the transfer, can take advantage of the situation and will take its time and might not give you good interest rates on the additional amount that you need to add to the mortgage (which does not include the transferred funds). Therefore, if the offer you receive is not an attractive one, it is recommended to check an alternative offer, at another bank for the full amount (the transferred mortgage + the addition you need for the new property).


The transfer process can be done in 2 ways,


If you already purchased an apartment then the bank transfers the mortgage to the new property and makes sure to update the collaterals accordingly.


If you have not yet purchased a new property but have already sold the old house then the bank transfers the mortgage into a bank guarantee, and the buyer of your old property deposits the money in favor of the guarantee in your bank and not directly for you. The payment for the mortgage will continue, and when you buy the new property, the bank will transfer the money from the guarantee to your seller and mortgage the new property.

Construction Inputs Index - Is there such an Index???

Many people do not know about the existence of the construction inputs index. After we understand what the construction inputs index is and to whom it relates, we will examine whether it really constitutes a significant threat in the differences when signing the contract and the actual payment to the contractor.


The construction inputs index measures how much it costs to building, and then determines the basket price. It measures the changes in the expenses that are required to purchase materials, products, etc., which are used to build residential buildings, and represents the composition of construction expenses of the contractors. This index is published and calculated every 15th of the month. The current basket price, relative to the price of the previous basket, reflects the increase in the index in percentages relative to the previous month.


The construction inputs index refers mainly to apartment purchasers from the contractor. These purchasers pay the cost of the apartment in installments (as appears in the purchase contract), and these payments are linked to the construction inputs index. This index has a huge impact on purchasers of new apartments and payments to the contractor may change scientifically, with amounts that were probably not taken into account before the acquisition.


That is why homebuyers should follow this measure to understand how much their debt has increased and to calculate whether they should pay the contractor early or delay payment as much as possible. Whether to advance the mortgage, or save in the meantime each month and transfer higher equity to the contractor? The question arises which is higher? Construction Input Index or Interest on the Mortgage? The purpose of buying the property is also significant when examining the feasibility of advancing or postponing payments to the contractor.


It is important to know that the construction inputs index is more expensive than the consumer price index. Try to ask the contractor for linkage to the consumer price index and not to the construction inputs index. This index also expresses the costs of the contractor. The construction inputs index does not reflect the costs of the land and the profit of the developer, but only the construction costs, but despite everything, the index is also calculated on them. There is a gap we are paying for, and unfortunately, the issue is still unresolved.

Tax.... Purchase ... And everything in between ..!

Purchase tax applies to the buyer and is paid for when purchasing an apartment. Purchase tax does not apply to profits and is paid to the tax authority when purchasing a real estate asset. The tax is paid on the investment itself, in accordance with the purchase tax brackets (as opposed to the betterment tax that applies to the seller when the asset is sold and calculated by an assessor).


The tax authorities understand that sometimes, before the buyer sells the previous apartment, he buys a new apartment, and then there is a certain period that he owns two apartments. This is a short term that constitutes an intermediate term until the sale date, and therefore the tax authorities exempt the buyer from purchase tax on the apartment if he sells the apartment within a year.


Purchase tax is paid according to the tax brackets. The higher the purchase transaction, the higher the tax and its rate. The purchase tax method allows for payment according to the scope of the transaction, so that in transactions for the purchase of apartments in a low volume, the purchase tax, respectively, is low and in many cases, there is no purchase tax.


Everyone pays purchase tax but if you buy a first apartment, or if you improve housing and sell the previous apartment within two years then the tax will be reduced relative to second / third apartment buyers and so on.


Purchase tax brackets are updated once a year on January 15th.


tax brackets when purchase a first apartment


0% on the part of the value up to 1,744,505 NIS.


3.5% on the scale between 1,744,505 NIS to 2,069,205 NIS.


5% on the scale between 2,069,205 NIS and 5,338,290 NIS.


8% on the scale between 5,338,290 and 17,794,305 NIS.


10% above NIS 17,794,305.


tax brackets when purchase a second apartment


The tax is paid when the buyer already owns an apartment and buys another apartment in his name. It is important to know - the purchaser of another apartment that sells an existing apartment within 24 months, will be charged with a lower purchase tax rate - as if this is his only apartment.


8% tax up to 5,194,225 NIS.


10% above 5,194,226 NIS.


Purchase tax on land / land is 6%. Any purchase of land or rights to a real estate that does not fall to the definition of an apartment, will be taxed. It is important to know that if a land is purchased and at the date of the purchase transaction, it has a building plan, allowing at least one residential building or land for which a building permit has been received for, within two years from the date of purchase of the land, then the purchase tax will be 5%.


Evacuation for construction plan - the apartment received by the tenant from the developer in the evacuation or construction area, in exchange for his old apartment in the complex – is exempt from purchase tax.


Transfer of property between spouses in a divorce proceeding - this action is not considered a real estate transaction and therefore there is no purchase tax on it.

Reverse mortgage

Have you reached the age you can begin? If you are 60 years old and you own a property then this mortgage is just for you!


The mortgage is for borrowers aged 60 to 94. You ask how it is possible? In all banks, the age limit for a mortgage amounts to 75 (80 if I exaggerate)


So, what is the logic and how does it happen?


It is very important to understand that a reverse mortgage is for the precious golden age who are interested in removing an existing mortgage / helping children / repaying loans / moving to protected housing / renovations, etc. (If I simplify it, then I mean the loan is for any purpose at large sums of money).


In this mortgage, the percentage of financing is determined according to the age of the borrowers and the asset must be in the name of the borrowers who live in the property. This loan does not check for income but only the property's condition. The loan is not conditional upon the purchase of life insurance and is subject to the approval of the financing body.


The loan is repayable in two ways: a balloon loan until the last of the spouses passes away, or a monthly payment of interest only linked to the CPI and monthly management fees. There is no repayment commission on the loan.


When the last borrower passes away, the heirs undertake, within one year, to repay the loan by taking a mortgage on the property or alternatively to sell the property and share the rest of the sale money after payment of the debt to the financing body.


It is important to understand that the very fact that you own an asset that can be mortgaged constitutes sufficient security for the lender to provide you with a loan for your personal needs.

Prepare yourselves for self- construction!

Have you decided to build your dream house? Congratulations!


Self-construction involves many uncertainties, and many decisions must be made, including dealing with bureaucracy and professionals. In order for the dream not to become a nightmare, it is important that you prepare correctly and carry out preliminary planning that will include unexpected expenses and enable you to make an efficient and correct financial decision. The difficulty with self-constructed is the ability to anticipate the amount that is required to pay in total. Estimating the costs will help determine the scope of the construction and the level of finishes to the house.


Prepare a plan that will be as close to reality as possible and that way the surprises in the continuation of construction will be minor. Prepare a plan that includes the cost of land, when buying land from the Israel Land Administration you will have to add the cost of land development, the construction cost (the cost of sq. meter X number of built square meter), and additional costs such as: purchase tax, betterment tax, fees for attorney, real-estate brokerage fees, financing costs, linking costs to the consumer price index or to the construction inputs index, delays in the timetables, fees and forms, the cost of construction supervisor, engineer, fees for architects and other professionals; the contents of the building: furniture, kitchen and sanitary equipment, etc.


In addition, there are one-time payments when buying the mortgage, such as appraisals, registration of collateral, fees for the bank, fees for a mortgage advisor, and if there are any irregularities in the budget, they affect the amount of the mortgage and the monthly repayment.


When purchasing the land, you are not required to present any building plans or permits to the bank, and the money will be paid directly to the contractor in one run. During the construction phase you will be required to present detailed plans for the house, contractor plans, construction supervisor, etc., and the funds will be transferred to your account gradually. The mortgage money will be released gradually according to the progress of the construction, according to an engineer's approval or up to the current amount approved by the Bank for self-construction.

The bank will always prefer to stay with a safety margin. The Bank is not obliged to approve, but usually will prefer to approve additional financing in order to have an asset in his favor, in respect of the credit.

Tama 38

Tama 38 is a national master plan for strengthening existing buildings against earthquakes, it was prepared at the initiative of the National Planning and Building Council, due to the location of the State of Israel on the Syrian-African Rift Line, which is at high risk of earthquakes.


The purpose of the plan is to provide an economic incentive to strengthen buildings (built before 1980) by granting increased building rights and shortening the bureaucratic procedures for obtaining building permits without the need to prepare a detailed plan, thereby encouraging homeowners to strengthen the structures they own.


This means strengthening the existing building and adding up to two floors (usually a regular floor and another floor for a penthouse or two roof apartments with an exit to the roof). In return, the tenants receive an additional room in the form of a security room and an elevator (buildings that have been allowed to be built after this date are subject to reinforcement instructions only without additional building rights).


As part of this project (TAMA 38/1), tenants enjoy many advantages, this is an original and quick way to improve the value of the property at no cost to the tenants, and all the expenses related to the execution of the project are paid by the developer. (Tama 38/ 2) the owner of the existing apartment receives a rent refund for the construction period until occupancy in the new apartment.


Despite the high importance of the project, TAMA 38 was implemented in a small way, mainly due to the entrepreneur's financing difficulties, and the construction of the buildings is subject to building rights on the roofs of the buildings. The project turned out to be complex, because the roofs cannot be mortgaged and if the entrepreneur enters difficulties and becomes insolvent, the project will not succeed and the bank will have no collateral to realize. (These are construction rights and not rights to the land itself, and therefore there is difficulty in providing collateral to the bank as a condition for financing the project).


This is a project that requires real professionalization in the field and it is very important to consult with the right professionals.

Purchase groups

A purchasing group is a group of individuals that together form a "partnership agreement" in order to purchase land and build a residential building on it, in order to reduce costs. A purchasing group can organize itself or through a management company that organizes all the stages of purchase and construction. The Group enables its members to reduce the brokerage gaps in purchasing the property as a finished product. On the other hand, the risk of participation in initiating the project is usually greater than the risk of buying one of the apartments, and the final price is not guaranteed. As stated, a purchasing group is an entrepreneurial entity in all respects, with all the risks and complications involved in real estate ventures.


Bank financing and a credit framework for the project as a whole are required, and each member of the group is examined individually to obtain approval from the lending bank. A member who has all the shareholders' equity required for the transaction must also open a credit facility at the lending bank. This is a safety net that will not create financing problems during the project.


The mortgage money of the members of the purchasing group is paid to the contractors and the professionals, according to the pace of the construction progress. The Bank treats each customer as a project entrepreneur and therefore the financing costs are high relative to a private person who takes out a mortgage.


The group locates the land, recruits the potential buyers, receives bids from the contractors and the professionals, and conducts them. The selection of professionals is very significant in a project of this type, and their choice should not be taken lightly. The cooperation between the parties is critical and decisive, and this is a complex process that requires in-depth financial planning from the beginning of the project.
